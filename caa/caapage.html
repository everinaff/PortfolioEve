Who We Are

We are committed to improving the life of every child with preventative healthcare and grassroots educational support in low-income communities.

How we got started

Since 2007, Children Across America has been working with local communities to ensure children in low-income families are receiving preventative medical care, and gaining literacy and leadership skills they need to succeed in school and in life.

Our Impact -- Helped more than 300 underserved families obtain healthcare coverage and connected their children with doctors and dentists within their local communities. By educating parents, more than 200 children have received annual check-ups and immunizations. Helped families fill more than 1,000 prescriptions at 50-75 percent below retail costs. Dozens of students have participated in its unique and fun book clubs and math sessions.

What we do

We empower communities so every child is given an opportunity to achieve academic excellence and protected against preventable illnesses.

Education Programs

EMPOWERING CHILDREN THROUGH EDUCATION
Every child benefits from opportunities that develop their reading and math skills. One student plus our unique education programs equals lasting success.
In addition to teaching children how to read, the literacy program focuses on making literature enjoyable and interactive. This results in children wanting to read more - even outside of school.
Our math program brings concepts to life. It offers a fun environment where the children can practice and discover the use of numbers in everyday life.

Healthcare Programs

PROVIDING ACCESS TO QUALITY HEALTHCARE.
Children Across America has helped hundreds of underserved families obtain healthcare coverage.
We connect children with doctors and dentists within their local communities. By educating parents, more than 200 children have received annual check-ups and immunizations. We've also works with low-income families to fill more than 1,000 prescriptions at 50-75 percent below retail costs.
Our organization aims to keep children, the world's future, healthy.
